## 1. Title tag rules

**Length:** Aim for 50–60 characters. Google truncates titles in the SERP based on pixel width (~580px), not character count, so short letters like `i` and `l` allow longer titles than wide ones like `W` and `M`. 55 characters is a safe target across most fonts.

**Front-load the keyword.** Google weights words at the start of the title slightly more, and users scanning a SERP read left-to-right (in left-to-right languages). Put the most important term first or close to it.

**One keyword, not five.** Stuffing dilutes intent and triggers spam patterns. Choose the one query you most want to win, and shape the title around that user's intent.

**Brand suffix logic.**
- If brand has equity in the niche → append it: `… | Bikehaus Freiburg`
- If brand is new or unknown → either omit, or append only on the homepage and category pages
- For ecommerce product pages → usually skip the brand suffix to maximize keyword space

**Avoid duplication across pages.** Every page on the site needs a unique title. Generic templates like `{product_name} - Buy Online` repeated across thousands of pages cause Google to rewrite titles itself, often badly.

**Match the page intent.** If the page is a how-to guide, the title should signal that (`How to…`, `Guide to…`, `Step-by-step…`). If it's a product, signal commerce (`Buy…`, `… for sale`, price hints).

## 2. Description rules

**Length:** 150–160 characters for desktop, 120 characters or less for mobile-first visibility. Google may pull a snippet from page content instead if the description doesn't match the query well.

**Not a ranking factor — but a major CTR factor.** Google has confirmed meta description doesn't directly affect ranking. It does heavily influence whether someone clicks. Treat it as ad copy.

**Include the keyword.** Google bolds matched query terms in the SERP description, which is a strong CTR signal. Include the primary keyword at least once, naturally.

**Lead with the user's outcome, not your features.** "Save 30% on commuter bikes shipped free in Germany" beats "We sell commuter bikes in our Freiburg shop".

**End with a CTA or hook.** "Shop now", "See the full guide", "Book your test ride", "Compare 12 models in 2 minutes" — give a reason to click.

**One unique description per page.** Same rule as titles. Duplicate descriptions get suppressed.

## 3. Title formulas (with examples)

Below, `{KW}` is the primary keyword, `{BRAND}` is the brand name, `{Y}` is the current year, `{N}` is a number, `{C}` is the city/location.

### F1. Direct + Modifier + Brand
`{KW} | {Modifier} | {BRAND}`
Example: `Mountain Bikes | Free Shipping in Germany | Bikehaus Freiburg`

Use when: ecommerce, transactional intent, brand has some equity.

### F2. Question
`{Question containing KW}? — {BRAND}`
Example: `Which Commuter Bike Fits Your City? — Bikehaus Freiburg`

Use when: informational intent, content marketing, competing in a busy SERP where you need to stand out.

### F3. Numbered Listicle
`{N} Best {KW} for {Audience} in {Y}`
Example: `12 Best Mountain Bikes for Beginners in 2026`

Use when: informational, comparison content. The number sets expectations and increases CTR notably.

### F4. How-To
`How to {Verb} {KW}: {Outcome}`
Example: `How to Choose a Mountain Bike: A Beginner's Guide`

Use when: tutorial intent, query starts with "how to" or "how do I".

### F5. Local
`{KW} in {C} | {Differentiator} | {BRAND}`
Example: `Bike Repair in Freiburg | Same-Day Service | Bikehaus`

Use when: local intent, brick-and-mortar business, local query keywords.

### F6. Versus / Comparison
`{A} vs. {B}: {Differentiator}`
Example: `Trek vs. Specialized: Which Mountain Bike Brand Wins in 2026?`

Use when: comparison/research intent, decision-stage queries.

### F7. Year-Anchored Update
`{KW}: {Y} {Modifier}`
Example: `Best Bike Helmets: 2026 Buyer's Guide`

Use when: the topic has a freshness signal. Update yearly.

## 4. Description formulas (with examples)

### D1. Outcome + Proof + CTA
`{Outcome the user wants}. {Proof point or differentiator}. {CTA}.`
Example: `Find your perfect commuter bike from 200+ models. Free fitting, 30-day returns, expert advice from Freiburg's largest bike shop. Browse the full range now.`

### D2. Question + Answer Tease
`{Question the user just searched}? {Brief answer + hook to read more}.`
Example: `Wondering which bike fits your commute? Compare 12 models by range, weight, and price — find your match in 2 minutes.`

### D3. Listicle Promise
`{Number} {Items} for {Audience}. {Inclusion criteria} {Update signal}.`
Example: `12 mountain bikes ranked for beginners — under €1500, tested for trail and city use. Updated for 2026 with new models from Trek, Giant, and Cube.`

### D4. Local Trust Stack
`{Service} in {City}. {Trust signal: years/reviews/badges}. {Distinctive offer}.`
Example: `Bike repairs in Freiburg since 2003. 4.9★ from 800+ customers. Same-day service for tune-ups, brake fixes, and gear adjustments. Walk-ins welcome.`

### D5. How-To Promise
`Learn how to {Outcome} in {Time/Steps}. {Who it's for} {Result}.`
Example: `Learn how to choose your first mountain bike in 5 simple steps. Built for beginners — by the end, you'll know exactly which size, type, and budget fits.`

## 5. Power words and modifiers

These are words that consistently lift CTR when used honestly (not clickbait). Pick what fits the page; never stack more than two.

**Trust / authority:** verified, expert, official, certified, trusted, proven, original

**Urgency / scarcity:** today, now, limited, last-chance, ending soon, while supplies last (use only if literally true)

**Specificity:** exact numbers, year, "step-by-step", "in {N} minutes", "without {pain point}"

**Curiosity:** the secret to, what nobody tells you, hidden, surprising, the truth about *(handle carefully — in many SERPs, restraint outperforms hype)*

**Value:** free, save, deal, discount, no-obligation, no hidden fees

**Outcome / benefit:** fast, easy, simple, instant, complete, ultimate, comprehensive

**Proximity (local):** near you, in {city}, local, nearby, same-day, walk-in

Avoid: "best ever", "amazing", "incredible", "you won't believe", "shocking" — these are saturated and read as low-quality in 2026 SERPs.

## 6. Language-specific notes

### German
- German words are typically 30% longer than English. Title character budget is the same (60 chars max), so titles fit fewer concepts. Often this means dropping the brand suffix to make room.
- Compound nouns (Fahrradhelm vs. "bike helmet") save space when used.
- Capitalize all nouns. Yes, even in titles. `Die besten Fahrräder` → `Die besten Fahrräder` (correct), not lowercased.
- Umlauts (ä, ö, ü, ß) display fine in modern SERPs. Don't substitute "ae", "oe" etc.
- Local searchers often query in mixed German + English (e.g., "Mountainbike kaufen Freiburg") — match the actual query language pattern.
- For German local pages, lead with city: `Freiburg` first establishes locality.

### French
- Accents (é, è, à) display correctly. Don't strip them.
- Polite phrasing in CTAs: "Découvrez" (discover) lands better than direct imperatives in many contexts.
- French is also 15–20% longer than English, similar challenge to German.

### Spanish
- Inverted question marks (¿) and exclamation points (¡) work in SERPs and can boost CTR for question-titled pages.
- Regional differences matter: vocabulary in es-ES (Spain) ≠ es-MX (Mexico). If targeting both, separate pages with hreflang.

### Japanese / Chinese
- Character count rules differ — pixel width is ~580px but each character is much wider. Aim for ~28–32 characters in title.
- No spaces; word segmentation is implicit.

### Arabic / Hebrew (RTL)
- Right-to-left rendering. Front-loaded keywords still apply but "front" is the right side of the line.
- Mixed Arabic + Latin numerals can render awkwardly; test in actual SERPs.

## 7. SERP-feature optimization

The SERP isn't just 10 blue links anymore. Tailor meta tags to capture features:

- **Featured snippet (Position 0):** for question-based queries, structure the page so the answer appears in a 40–60 word block right after an H2 that matches the question. Title should mirror the question wording.
- **People Also Ask (PAA):** including FAQ schema on the page surfaces your domain in PAA boxes for related queries — even when you don't rank organically for them.
- **Sitelinks:** automatic. Earn them by having a clean site structure and a strong brand-search volume. Can't be directly requested.
- **Rich results (Product, Recipe, Article, Event, etc.):** require schema markup, not meta tag changes — but the title/description still control how the rich result reads.
- **AI Overviews / SGE:** Google's AI-generated summaries cite sources. Structured content with clear topical authority and explicit answers is most likely to be cited.

## 8. Diagnosing why a current meta tag is failing

When the user shows you an existing title/description with poor CTR, run through this diagnostic:

1. **Length issue?** Is it being truncated in the SERP? (Test by searching the exact title phrase on Google.)
2. **Intent mismatch?** Does the title promise what the user actually wants for that query?
3. **Keyword absent or buried?** Is the primary keyword present and front-loaded?
4. **Generic / template-y?** Does it look the same as 9 competitors?
5. **No hook?** Is there any reason to click *this* result vs. another?
6. **Duplication?** Is the same title used on many pages of the site?
7. **Google rewriting?** If the title in the SERP differs from what's in the HTML, Google has overridden it. Usually means the original title was off-topic, too long, or duplicated. A better-written title will be respected.

## 9. The 3-variant deliverable

Every meta-rewrite request should output **three variants**, clearly labeled, with character counts shown. Never just propose one — the user needs to pick what fits their voice.

**Format:**

```
Variant A — Direct
Title: Mountain Bikes for Beginners | Bikehaus Freiburg (52)
Description: Find your first mountain bike from 60+ beginner-friendly models. Expert fitting, free shipping in Germany, 30-day returns. (139)

Variant B — Benefit-led
Title: Find Your First Mountain Bike — Expert Help in Freiburg (54)
Description: New to mountain biking? Our Freiburg-based experts match you with the right bike in under 30 minutes. Free fitting, lifetime tune-ups. (143)

Variant C — Specificity / curiosity
Title: 12 Beginner Mountain Bikes Tested in 2026 — Bikehaus (51)
Description: We tested 12 entry-level mountain bikes for fit, durability, and value. See which ones we recommend for first-time riders this year. (138)

Recommendation: Variant B — strongest local + benefit hook, and your "expert fitting" differentiator is unique vs. the 3 competitors currently outranking this page. But A is safer if you want minimum risk of underperforming the current title.
```

Always close with a recommendation, but make clear it's a recommendation, not a verdict.
